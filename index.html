About

Mr. Pritts currently is a Post-Doctoral Research Scientist at Facebook Reality Labs, where he is responsible for conducting novel research in computer vision. He also serves as adjunct adviser for The Machine Learning Lab at Ukrainian Catholic University in Lviv. Previously Mr. Pritts worked at The Czech Institute for Informatics, Robotics, and Cybernetics (CIIRC) and the Visual Recognition Group (VRG) at Czech Technical University in Prague. He is the recipient of the ACCV 2018 Saburo Tsuji Best Paper Award and the ICVNZ 2013 Best Paper Award. Mr. Pritts’s research focuses on modeling repeated content in images and multi-model estimation, and he is published in the leading research conferences and journals for computer vision. Prior to his work at CTU in Prague, he was Lead Engineer at BAE Systems, where he contributed to several US Department of Defense (DARPA) computer-vision research efforts, and at NASA he created prototype gesture-recognition software for the International Space Station. Mr. Pritts received his B.Sc. in Mathematics at The University of North Texas and his M.Sc. in Computer Science from Czech Technical University in Prague with distinction. His thesis was awarded the prestigious Dean’s prize.
